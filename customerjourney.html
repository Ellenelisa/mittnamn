Customer Jounrney

Mapping a custumors journey from interviews and observations.

Client

School project

Time

2 weeks

Project

In this school project, we developed a comprehensive customer journey focused on a specific target group, which, in our case, was adults with limited financial resources. Our objective was to identify pain points and explore potential enhancements to enhance the overall customer experience. Through this project, we aimed to gain insights into the challenges faced by individuals in this demographic and to propose solutions that could lead to a more positive and efficient customer journey.

Research

To better understand our users' needs, goals, and behaviors, we conducted a survey among individuals who align with our target group. We received 15 responses, which provided us with valuable insights into our users. These insights enabled us to create a basic persona, allowing us to develop a more informed understanding of our target audience.

Our key insights from this was:

1. Our users want to have a sturucterd store with many alternativs.

2. It has to be easy to both go to the store and the shopping itself.

3. They choose by price and quality.

Obeservations

To gather more information about our users' behavior and emotions while they are in a grocery store, we conducted four observational sessions accompanied by complementary semi-structured interviews. This approach allowed us to gain deeper insights into how our users navigate and experience the grocery store environment.

To ensure that our test subjects were operating under uniform conditions, we developed a checklist for all team members to adhere to during the observation sessions

During the observation sessions, our team consisted of two key roles: a moderator and an observer. The moderator's responsibility was to document the observations and pose supplementary questions to gain a more comprehensive understanding of the users' behavior. Simultaneously, the observer documented the proceedings to ensure that no crucial details were overlooked during the process.

After completing all the observations, each team member analyzed their respective recordings and created their own individualized customer journey. This approach allowed us to facilitate easy comparison and identify common patterns and elements that were instrumental in developing the final customer journey.

The steps we used to build our customer journey:

Activity - What they were doing

Goal - What they are trying to accomplish

Picture - Picture that shows the acitivty

Touchpoint - Where they were located

Feelings - How they feel in the situation

Thoughts - What they are thinking in the situation

Pain points - The aspects that frustuate them

Improvemnts - Potential improvements to adress the pain points.

Result

For the primary customer journey, we opted to highlight situations in which the user displayed strong emotions, whether they were positive or negative.

Insights

The first thing that comes to mind is how challenging it can be to conduct observations, essentially becoming 'the fly on the wall.' The second thing is that it's incredibly rewarding and enlightening. I learned a great deal about my test subject and their behavior by shadowing them in the store. It provided insights that I believe I would never have gained solely through surveys and interviews. People often have a perception of what they do and how they behave that doesn't always align with their actual actions in reality.
